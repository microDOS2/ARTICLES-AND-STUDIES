Microdosing Research & Commentary

An overview of recent articles, studies, and anecdotal reports on the topic of microdosing psychedelics.

Expert Commentary & Articles

Microdosing: Everything You Need to Know (Verywell Health)

A reader-friendly primer exploring how microdosing works, potential benefits like stress reduction and mood improvement, alongside risks and legal considerations.

Believers say microdosing psychedelics helps them. Scientists are trying to measure the claims (AP News)

Balances heartfelt personal stories with scientific caution, highlighting that many reported benefits may stem from placebo effects.

Personal Stories & Anecdotal Reports

Motherhood on Mushrooms: Microdosing Psilocybin Helped Me Cope with Parenting Stress (SELF)

A candid essay by a single mom who used 0.15 g psilocybin gummies every other day, reporting less anxiety, better clarity, and a more grounded presence—especially during parenting.

After 20 Years in the Finance Industry I Felt Depressed and Anxious... (Business Insider)

A finance veteran describes how microdosing psilocybin during the pandemic sparked newfound calm, helped overcome self-doubt, and eventually inspired a coaching and book venture.

Could a taboo treatment help struggling new moms? (San Francisco Chronicle)

Covers a major psilocybin-like treatment trial for postpartum depression, exposing regulatory hurdles, gender‑bias in drug research, and cultural taboos surrounding psychedelics.

Scientific Studies & Reviews

Adults who microdose psychedelics report health related motivations... (Scientific Reports, 2021)

A large cross-sectional study (n≈4,000) showing that microdosers report lower anxiety and depression and often aim to improve wellness—though the design limits claims of causation.

The therapeutic potential of microdosing psychedelics in... (PMC, 2020)

Overview of short-term benefits—like improved mood and social connection—and standard microdosing ranges such as 10–20 µg LSD or 0.3–0.5 g psilocybin mushrooms.

Psychedelic microdosing benefits and challenges: an empirical codebook (Harm Reduction Journal, 2019)

A systematic gathering of community-reported pros (e.g., improved mood, focus) and cons (e.g., anxiety, physical discomfort) among 278 microdosers.

A systematic study of microdosing psychedelics (PLOS One, 2019)

A six-week observational study found boosts in psychological functioning on dosing days, with modest drops in depression and stress—but also noted strong expectancy bias among users.

Is microdosing a placebo? A rapid review of low-dose LSD (PMC, 2024)

Suggests microdosing can affect neurobiology, mood, and subjective experience—but also raises concerns about placebo effects.

Self-blinding citizen science to explore psychedelic microdosing (eLife, 2021)

An open-source, self-blinding study design that highlights how expectations may drive reported benefits, offering a possible blueprint for future placebo-controlled citizen studies.

Can microdosing psychedelics make one feel more authentic? (PMC, 2023)

A small longitudinal study in the Netherlands (n=18) tracking “state authenticity”—participants reported feeling more true to themselves and more engaged in satisfying activities on microdosing days.
